Problem Statement

With VH360째, we're looking to solve the problem of individuals losing touch with their high schools both during attendance and after they graduate. Core memories are formed during these years, and there should be a place to share, view, and reflect on them that's personalized to the specific school. What makes VH360째 even more unique compared to popular social media sites is the fact that it's ad-free and isn't focused on content performance. It's simply an archive reserved for students, which must be proven using credentials. This gives it a school-centric, preservationist style.

AI Approach

We plan to integrate AI into VH360째 for content moderation. Our goal is to share appropriate, safe memories, and we intend to use a video analysis tool, like TensorFlow or Hugging Face Transformers. While neither of us have exprience with these tools, we believe this goal is feasible because of the tools' popularity and widespread documentation.

Phase 1: Research

During this phase, we were mainly focused on deciding exactly what we wanted to create. We knew something with video was interesting to us and we love capturing memories. Therefore, an archive of videos from our high school was the perfect option. From there, we decided something like Vimeo would be better than something like YouTube/Twitch because we wanted it to be more of a file storage website than an algorithm-based feed. Throughout our research, we came to some key conclusions. First, we figured out that we wanted to use H.264 (AVC) for video compression. This can then be paired with Handbrake or FFmpeg for encoding. Additionally, we decided that AWS and their S3 service would be best for hosting our website when we want it to go live. Finally, we came to conclusions on both the front-end and back-end of our website. The front-end will consist of HTML, CSS, and JS. The back-end will be either Django or Flask. We've started a rough draft of our website. It's very similar to YouTube at this time as that is one of our biggest inspirations.

Target Audience

This website, or project, is intended to be used by parents, students, and staff of Vernon Hills High School. It offers a unique and interactive way to stay connected with each other and their school.

Final Product

In terms of a final product, the goal is to have a visually-appealing, fully functional website that displays content in both an archive and feed format. Users can also contact the makers of the project (us) and send photos or videos to be published to the site (after careful review). An auto-generated email will respond to their request and another will send us their submission. The entirety of the website will be up-to-date, accurate, and both useful and ejoyable for the users.
